**What if your language model isn't generating text — it's settling into a memory?** A new paper from ICLR 2026 reframes LLM inference through the lens of statistical physics, and the results are both beautiful and practically useful.

**What it is:** The paper "Reasoning as Attractor Dynamics" (Awadhiya, 2026) proposes that transformer-based LLMs should be understood as **Dense Associative Memories** — not autoregressive text machines. Every reasoning chain a model produces corresponds to a trajectory through an energy landscape. *Correct answers* fall into **flat, wide attractor basins** (stable memories), while *hallucinations* are **sharp, unstable local minima** — they look deep, but they're narrow and brittle. Inference is then modelled as a relaxation process toward equilibrium, not greedy next-token prediction.

**Why it matters:** This framing does two things at once. First, it gives hallucinations a *geometric diagnosis* — they live in sharp, perturbation-sensitive regions of the energy landscape. Second, it provides a practical fix that requires *no fine-tuning*: at test time, sample $K$ reasoning paths, compute the energy of each (approximated as length-normalised negative log-likelihood), and reweight them with a Gibbs/Boltzmann measure. Low-energy trajectories (wide basins) win.

$$P_{\text{retrieval}}(y^{(k)}) \propto \exp\!\left(-\beta \cdot H(y^{(k)})\right)$$

where the paper uses an **inverse-square sharpening** scheme:

$$w_k \propto \frac{1}{E(y^{(k)})^2 + \epsilon}$$

This is essentially **simulated annealing at test time** — hot particles (hallucinatory paths with high entropy) evaporate; cold particles (resonant, low-entropy trajectories) dominate.

**Quick example or insight:** On GSM8K (grade-school math), applied to Microsoft's tiny Phi-3.5-mini (3.8B parameters):

```python
# Conceptual sketch — full impl in the paper
def gibbs_select(paths, model, beta=2.0, eps=1e-6):
    """
    paths: list of K reasoning chains (strings)
    Returns: the answer with highest Gibbs-weighted vote
    """
    energies = [avg_nll(model, p) for p in paths]   # E(y) = mean token NLL
    weights  = [1.0 / (e**2 + eps) for e in energies]
    total    = sum(weights)
    votes    = {}
    for p, w in zip(paths, weights):
        ans = extract_answer(p)
        votes[ans] = votes.get(ans, 0) + w / total
    return max(votes, key=votes.get)
```

| Strategy | Accuracy |
|---|---|
| Greedy decoding | 78.4% |
| Standard sampling (K=12) | 84.7% |
| **Gibbs-weighted (K=12)** | **90.1%** |

That's +11.6% over greedy and +5.4% over naïve ensemble — all from a physics-inspired reweighting step, no gradient update required.

**The deep connection:** The Gibbs retrieval operator turns out to approximate the update rule of **Modern Hopfield Networks**, a class of energy-based associative memories with exponential storage capacity. The LLM is the memory; inference is retrieval; the energy landscape is real.
