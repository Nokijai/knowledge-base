## Time Series

A **time series** is a sequence of observations ordered in time: $x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_T$. In finance we work with **prices** (levels) or **returns** (changes).

$$
X_t = \mu_t + \varepsilon_t
$$

For **log prices**:

$$
p_t = \ln(P_t), \quad r_t = p_t - p_{t-1} = \ln\!\left(\frac{P_t}{P_{t-1}}\right)
$$

> Think of a time series as a **movie of one variable**, not a bag of random numbers. Order matters — today's price depends on yesterday's.

---

## Stationarity

A series is **stationary** when its mean, variance, and autocovariance are stable over time. Non-stationary series drift or trend; naive regression on two trending prices produces **spurious correlation**.

**Weak stationarity** requires for all lags $k$:

$$
\mathbb{E}[X_t] = \mu, \quad \mathrm{Var}(X_t) = \sigma^2, \quad \mathrm{Cov}(X_t, X_{t-k}) = \gamma_k
$$

A **random walk** violates this — its variance grows with $t$:

$$
X_t = X_{t-1} + \varepsilon_t, \quad \varepsilon_t \sim \text{i.i.d.}
$$

> Stationary = fluctuates around a fixed home (thermostat at 72 °F). Non-stationary = drunk walk with no fixed home and growing variance.

## Integration Order — I(0) and I(1)

| Order | Meaning | Finance example |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| **I(0)** | Stationary (or stationary after simple transform) | Returns |
| **I(1)** | Non-stationary in levels; first difference ≈ I(0) | Asset prices |

$$
\Delta X_t = X_t - X_{t-1} \sim I(0) \quad \text{when } X_t \sim I(1)
$$

Two I(1) series may share a long-run link — that is **cointegration**.

> **I(1)** = sticky memory (today = yesterday + shock). **I(0)** = short memory (shocks die out). Pairs trading assumes the spread is I(0).

## Augmented Dickey–Fuller (ADF) Test

The standard first step before ARMA modeling or claiming mean reversion.

- **H₀:** Unit root (non-stationary)
- **H₁:** Stationary
- **Rule:** p-value < 0.05 → reject H₀ → treat as stationary

$$
\Delta X_t = \alpha + \beta t + \gamma X_{t-1} + \sum_{i=1}^{p} \delta_i \Delta X_{t-i} + u_t
$$

$$
H_0: \gamma = 0 \quad \text{vs.} \quad H_1: \gamma < 0
$$

```python
from statsmodels.tsa.stattools import adfuller
stat, pvalue, lags, nobs, crit, icbest = adfuller(series.dropna())
```

> ADF asks: *"Does the change pull the series back toward a level, or is it pure noise on a drifting walk?"*

## Differencing and Log-Diff

**Differencing** removes trends and converts I(1) → I(0). **Log** stabilizes scale.

$$
\Delta X_t = X_t - X_{t-1}
$$

**Log-diff on a ratio** $R_t = Q_t / V_t$:

$$
\Delta g_t = \ln R_t - \ln R_{t-1} = \ln\!\left(\frac{R_t}{R_{t-1}}\right) \approx \frac{R_t - R_{t-1}}{R_{t-1}}
$$

> **Levels** = where the ratio is. **Log-diff** = how fast it changed today — speed vs position.

---

## ACF and PACF

- **ACF** — correlation between $X_t$ and $X_{t-k}$ (includes indirect paths).
- **PACF** — direct correlation at lag $k$ after removing shorter-lag effects.

$$
\rho_k = \frac{\mathrm{Cov}(X_t, X_{t-k})}{\mathrm{Var}(X_t)}
$$

```python
from statsmodels.graphics.tsaplots import plot_acf, plot_pacf
plot_acf(series.dropna(), lags=30)
plot_pacf(series.dropna(), lags=30)
```

> Slow ACF decay → long memory / non-stationary. Sharp PACF drop after lag 1 → AR(1) candidate on the stationary transform.

## Correlation vs Stationarity vs Cointegration

| Idea | What it checks | Pairs-trading relevance |
|------|----------------|-------------------------|
| **Correlation** | Short-run co-movement | High corr does **not** guarantee mean reversion |
| **Stationarity** | Single series has a stable mean | Spread/ratio must be I(0) for z-score rules |
| **Cointegration** | Long-run equilibrium between two I(1) series | The actual "leash" test for pairs |

> **Correlation** = two runners often move together. **Cointegration** = tied by a leash so their gap is stable even though each path wanders.

---

## Practical Checklist

1. Plot **levels** and **ratio**; compute **correlation**
2. Run **ADF** on the trading signal series
3. If non-stationary → try **log-diff** or test the **spread**
4. Inspect **ACF/PACF** on the series you will actually model
5. Only then: z-score, ARMA, or backtest — document H₀/H₁ and p-values
