## Objective

Test whether **QQQ** (Nasdaq-100) and **VOO** (S&P 500) share a long-run cointegration relationship, and assess feasibility of a mean-reversion pairs strategy.

**Data:** Yahoo Finance, 2020-01-01 → 2026-05-01

## Results

| Test | Result | Interpretation |
|------|--------|----------------|
| **Correlation** | > 0.95 | Strong co-movement |
| **ADF — ratio** | p ≈ 0.10, non-stationary | Mean-reversion ARMA on ratio levels is **not valid** |
| **ADF — log-diff** | p ≈ 0, stationary | Differenced ratio usable for short-horizon modeling |
| **Engle–Granger** | p ≈ 0.21, not cointegrated | No stable long-run equilibrium |

---

## Key Insights

**Strong sync, weak cointegration.** High correlation does not imply tradable spread mean reversion. QQQ has structurally outperformed VOO since 2020 — the ratio **trends** rather than oscillates around a fixed mean.

**If trading the ratio:** model on log-diff or detrended stationary series with rolling windows, transaction costs, and position limits — not a static z-score alone.

**Pairs verdict:** At 5% significance we fail to reject no cointegration. QQQ–VOO is **not a strict cointegration pair** in this sample. Any strategy should be framed as **relative-value / trending ratio**, not classic cointegration pairs.
